Golf Handicap Estimator

Don't know your handicap? Enter what you typically shoot and get an instant estimate — no membership, course ratings, or score history required.

How this estimator works

An official golf handicap requires posting scores with exact course ratings and slope values over many rounds. But most casual golfers just want a solid ballpark answer to "what's my handicap?" This estimator uses the same underlying math as the official World Handicap System — converting each score into a differential based on course difficulty — but substitutes sensible defaults for the data you don't have. The fewer scores you enter, the wider your estimated range, because a single round tells us much less about your true ability than five rounds do.

What your handicap actually means

A handicap represents your demonstrated scoring potential, not your average score. A 20-handicap golfer doesn't shoot 20-over every round — they shoot 20-over on a good day. That's why official handicaps use only your best 8 of your last 20 rounds. Our estimate accounts for this by weighting your better scores more heavily than your worse ones.
