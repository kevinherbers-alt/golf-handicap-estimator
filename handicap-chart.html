How to read this chart

This chart approximates the relationship between what you typically shoot and your handicap on a course of standard difficulty. Because an official handicap is based on your best recent rounds — not your average — your true handicap is usually a few strokes lower than your average score minus par. That's already reflected in the numbers above.

Your actual handicap also depends on the difficulty of the courses you play: shooting 95 on a hard course indicates a better golfer than shooting 95 on an easy one. For a personalized number that accounts for course difficulty, use our handicap estimator, or if you track your rounds with course ratings, the official WHS calculator.

What's a good handicap?

Most golfers who maintain an official handicap fall somewhere between 10 and 25. A single-digit handicap puts you in roughly the top quarter of players who track their game, and scratch (0) or better is rare — well under 2% of golfers. But because casual players who don't keep a handicap tend to score higher, a mid-teens handicap is genuinely better than the average golfer overall.
