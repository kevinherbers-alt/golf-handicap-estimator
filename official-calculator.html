How the official calculation works

Under the World Handicap System, each round you post becomes a "score differential" using the formula: (your score − course rating) × 113 ÷ slope rating. The 113 represents the slope of a course of standard difficulty. Your handicap index is then the average of your best 8 differentials from your most recent 20 rounds. When you have fewer than 20 rounds, the system uses a smaller number of your best scores — and applies a small adjustment in some cases — which this calculator handles automatically.

Course rating and slope are printed on your scorecard, usually near the tee information. Course rating estimates what a scratch golfer would shoot; slope measures how much harder the course plays for a bogey golfer than a scratch golfer.

How many rounds do you need?

An official handicap index can be established with as few as 3 rounds (54 holes). The more rounds you enter here, the more accurate and stable your index becomes. If you don't have course ratings handy or only remember rough scores, start with our quick estimator instead.

This calculator follows WHS formulas but is not a substitute for an official handicap index from an authorized golf association.
