Course Handicap Calculator

Convert your handicap index into the number of strokes you actually get at a specific course and tee.

Why your handicap changes by course

Your handicap index is a portable measure of your ability, but the number of strokes you actually receive changes depending on where you play. A hard course (high slope) gives you more strokes; an easy one gives you fewer. The official formula is: course handicap = handicap index × (slope ÷ 113) + (course rating − par). The result is rounded to the nearest whole number — that's how many strokes you get for that round.

Where to find slope and rating

Every rated course prints these numbers on the scorecard, usually in a small table near the tee names. Each set of tees has its own course rating and slope, so make sure you use the values for the tees you're actually playing.
