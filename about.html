About Golf Handicap Estimator

Golf Handicap Estimator exists because the most common handicap question — "what's my handicap?" — is asked most often by golfers who have the least data to answer it. Official handicap calculators assume you've been posting scores with course ratings and slope values for 20 rounds. Most casual golfers just know they usually shoot "around 95."

Our estimator is built for exactly that golfer: enter whatever scores you remember, describe your course in plain terms, and get an honest estimated range — along with what that number actually means compared to other players.

For golfers who do track their rounds, our official calculator implements the full World Handicap System formula, including score differentials and the best-8-of-20 selection rules with proper adjustments for players with fewer posted rounds. And our course handicap converter answers the question that comes up on the first tee: "how many strokes do I get today?"

Every tool here is free, loads instantly, and works on any device. The estimates are honest about their uncertainty — an estimated handicap is presented as a range, not false precision — and none of them are a substitute for an official handicap index issued through an authorized golf association, which requires posting scores through a licensed club or association.
